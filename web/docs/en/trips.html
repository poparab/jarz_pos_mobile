Delivery Trips

Group multiple ready orders into a delivery trip, assign a courier, send the trip out, and mark it delivered when the courier returns.

When to use: When you have multiple delivery orders ready to go out together with one courier. Also used to track individual deliveries if only one order is assigned per trip.

Part A — Creating a Delivery Trip

Trips are created from the Kanban Board. The Trips screen then tracks them.

1

Go to the Kanban Board and find Ready orders

Orders in the Ready column that are prepared for delivery can be added to a trip.

2

Select invoices for the trip

Long-press an order card (or tap the multi-select checkbox if available) to select it. Select all orders that will go in this trip.

3

Tap Create Trip

The Create Delivery Trip dialog opens. It shows the selected orders, total amount, and total shipping.

4

Select a courier

Tap Select Courier and choose from the list. The courier's current outstanding balance is shown next to their name — check it against Courier Balances if needed.

5

Add notes (optional) and confirm

Add any special notes in the Notes field. Tap Create Trip to save. The trip is created and the orders move to Out for Delivery on the Kanban.

Part B — Managing Trips from the Trips Screen

6

Open the Delivery Trips screen

Tap Delivery Trips from the side menu. You see two tabs: Active (trips in progress) and Completed (delivered trips).

7

Tap a trip card to view details

The trip detail view shows all orders in the trip, the assigned courier, timestamps, total amount, and total shipping.

8

Send for Delivery (if not already done)

If the trip has just been created and has not yet been dispatched, tap Send for Delivery. The confirmation "Trip sent for delivery" appears and all orders in the trip move to Out for Delivery.

9

Mark the trip as Delivered

When the courier returns and all orders have been delivered, tap Mark as Delivered on the trip. All orders move to Delivered on the Kanban. The trip moves to the Completed tab in the Trips screen.

💡 Tips

  • Group orders with the same delivery area into one trip to reduce courier trips.
  • Check the courier's balance before assigning — a courier with a large outstanding balance may need to settle first.
  • If an order is wrongly included in a trip, contact your manager to remove it before dispatching.

⚠️ Common Issues

  • Trip not appearing in Active tab: Refresh the Trips screen. The trip may still be syncing.
  • Cannot select invoices in Kanban: The multi-select mode may require a long-press on the first card. Try again.
  • "No couriers available": The courier list is empty — ask your manager to add couriers in the system.
