Courier Balances

A read-only view of the outstanding cash balance for each courier — how much each courier owes us (or we owe them).

When to use: Before assigning a new delivery trip, to check if a courier has an unusually large outstanding balance that should be settled first.

Understanding the Balances Screen

The Courier Balances screen lists every active courier along with three balance figures:

Courier Pays Us

Cash the courier collected from customers and has not yet handed back to the branch.

Pay Courier

Money we owe the courier (e.g. for delivery fees or advances).

Net Balance

The net position — positive means courier owes us money; negative means we owe the courier.

The Invoices count shows how many unsettled deliveries are linked to each courier.

How to Open Courier Balances

1

Open via the Kanban menu

On the Kanban Board, tap the ☰ Menu icon → Courier Balances. The balances overlay appears.

2

Review each courier's balance

The table shows all couriers. Look at the Net column. A high positive net means the courier is holding a lot of customer cash that has not been handed back.

3

Close the overlay

Tap outside the overlay or the X button to return to the Kanban Board. This screen is read-only for staff — no actions can be taken from here. Settlement is performed by managers.

ℹ️ Staff Role Note

As a staff member, you can view courier balances but you cannot settle them. Settlement (creating a payment against the courier) is a manager action. If you see an unusually large balance, inform your manager.

💡 Tips

  • Check courier balances before creating a new delivery trip to make sure the courier you want to assign is in good standing.
  • If "No couriers found" is displayed, the courier list has not been configured. Contact your manager.

⚠️ Common Issues

  • Balances not loading: The screen may be fetching. Wait a few seconds; if it keeps failing, check internet connection.
  • Courier balance looks wrong: It may reflect recently delivered but not yet settled trips. Inform your manager.
